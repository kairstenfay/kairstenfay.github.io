About Me

Data scientist, biologist, investor, teacher, and traveler

I am a passionate problem solver and a highly driven self-learner with a logic-forward mind and a desire to know the truth. As a data scientist, I want to help solve many of the worlds biggest problems, from economic growth and development to public health, environmental issues, and more.

I am an independent student studying data science full-time in a DIY-style bootcamp. I use Coursera modules, Kaggle competitions, and other resources to complete projects and learn core concepts.

Outside of data science, I have a lot of other interests. I'm passionate about the environment, and I studied biology with a concentration in ecology, evolution, and conservation in my undergraduate career. I've worked as both a lab and a field technician previously, performing tasks ranging from capturing butterflies with nets to removing mosquitoes heads to collect dog heartworm parasites. I enjoy investing money in the stock market in my free time, especially in socially responsible funds. I'm a savvy self-starter, and have even dabbled in some small entrepreneurial pursuits, such as selling handmade earrings through my Etsy shop.

I am self-aware and have previously tutored subjects ranging from calculus and chemistry to English. I am patient, understanding of diverse personality types, and work wonderfully well on a team. If you are in the Seattle area, you can hire me, too! Thanks for visiting my website, and I hope you enjoy my posts.
