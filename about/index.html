About Me

I received my Bachelor's degree in 2015 in Biology with a concentration in Ecology, Evolution and Conservation. For over a year now, I have been working on a GIS project that analyzes the coat-color phenology (i.e. global timing) of 20 mammal and bird species that change coat color from brown to white in the winter. This experience led me to develop skills in Python and R. I realized that, although cleaning up data may be tedious at times, I thoroughly enjoyed using programming and statistics skills to visualize data and answer unsolved questions. I am now actively pursuing a career in data science or a closely related field such as data analytics.
